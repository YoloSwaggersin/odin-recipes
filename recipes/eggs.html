Scrambled Eggs

Eggs might be the first thing anyone learns how to cook. I remember it was for me, and I somehow thought
it would be a good idea to place my entire palm on the hot stove top. Funny times, awful smell. That might
or might not have been my moment of waking up in the world. Anyway, it's so easy to do this one! Maybe the
hardest part for a beginner would be making sure not to get any egg shell in the finished product, but if
you're having trouble, always remember that the internet is like a huge library. Someone has written
something on how to break an egg perfectly every time. Butter that pan, crack those eggs in, and don't
forget to throw the cheese in right before you take it out so that it doesn't burn.

Ingredients

  • eggs
  • butter
  • cheese
  • confidence

Numbered Steps

  1. break eggs in bowl, mix yolk in
  2. heat up pan, butter inside when warm
  3. pour in eggs, occasionally stirring to prevent burning
  4. before they're completely solid, add cheese
  5. remove at the first sight of browning
